# ⏳ Countdown to Freedom

This is a personal countdown timer ticking down to **1 March 2029, 00:00 UTC** — the date my current employment contract expires.

## What's this about?

I'm currently under a contract with a retention clause. Translation: if I leave before **1 March 2029**, I have to pay a penalty fee to my employer. So I'm not leaving early. I'm just... watching the clock. Very closely.

When that counter hits zero, I'm free — no penalties, no strings, no awkward HR conversations about reimbursements.

## The countdown modes

The app has a few ways to watch time bleed away:

- **Default** — the classic breakdown: years, months, days, hours, minutes, and seconds. The full picture.
- **Months** — for when you want to feel like it's not _that_ far away.
- **Weeks** — for a more granular sense of dread or hope, depending on the day.
- **Days** — the real unit of suffering/anticipation.
- **Hours** — for the final stretch, when every hour counts.

All single-unit modes show decimals, so yes, you can watch `1,461.99998` days slowly become `1,461.99997`.

## How to use it

Just open `countdown.html` in any browser. No server needed, no dependencies, no install. It's a single HTML file that runs entirely in your browser.

## Why does this exist?

Because staring at a number going down is weirdly motivating. Also because spreadsheets felt too corporate for something this personal.

## What happens on 1 March 2029?

The counter hits zero. I close my laptop. I update my LinkedIn. Life goes on — just somewhere else, penalty-free. 🎉
